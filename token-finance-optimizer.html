Why Chunking Strategy Matters

Smaller chunks increase retrieval precision — the vector similarity search returns tighter semantic matches. However, they raise per-chunk overhead and may require higher K to cover a topic, partially offsetting savings.

Overlap preserves sentence continuity across chunk boundaries, preventing mid-thought truncation. Every overlapping token is however duplicated in cost. In compliance contexts, the continuity value justifies the cost. In cost-optimized pipelines, overlap should be minimized or replaced with metadata anchors.

Top-K is the primary driver of context window load. K=8 with 512-token chunks produces 4,096 context tokens before system and user prompts are counted. Reducing K from 10 to 4 on a 2,500 call/day pipeline typically yields 30–45% cost reduction.

Structured Prompt Engineering

System instructions should be treated as a compiled, cached object — stable across sessions and compressed to the minimum viable specification. Every token in the system prompt is paid for on every call. A 400-token reduction in system prompt at 2,500 calls/day at $3/M tokens equals $1.09 saved daily, or ~$400/year.

Sentence window chunking indexes at the sentence level but retrieves a surrounding window, improving coherence without the token cost of large fixed-size chunks. It is particularly effective for regulatory and compliance document retrieval.

Semantic caching stores embedding-similar queries with their responses. A 30% cache hit rate on context-heavy queries effectively reduces billed tokens by 25–30% on the cached portion, with infrastructure overhead well below model inference cost.
