Critical Gaps
No per-invocation regulatory reference tagging

CRITICAL GAPS
Critical Gaps
AI involvement not in SOX attestation
Section 302 certification must cover material controls. If AI materially influences financial reporting, omitting it from the attestation is a misstatement risk.
Insufficient access controls on AI system
SOX requires documented access controls on all systems that affect financial reporting. AI inference endpoints must be in scope.
Change management gap: AI config changes not controlled
Uncontrolled changes to AI prompts, models, or parameters in financial reporting contexts are an IT General Control deficiency.

Basel III Readiness Score
CRITICAL GAPS
Critical Gaps
AI not in model risk register
Basel Pillar 2 requires all risk-relevant models to be inventoried and risk-tiered. AI systems producing credit or market risk outputs must be registered.
No independent model validation for AI
SR 11-7 and equivalent Basel guidance require independent validation of models used in risk functions. Internal validation by model developers is insufficient.
Model limitations not documented for users
Personnel relying on AI outputs must understand the assumptions, limitations, and failure modes. Undocumented limitations create interpretive risk.

Critical Gaps
AI vendor not in ICT risk management framework
DORA requires all ICT risks including AI vendor risks to be managed under a documented framework. Absence is a direct regulatory breach for EU financial entities from Jan 2025.
Contractual provisions insufficient for DORA Art. 30
DORA specifies mandatory contractual provisions for ICT providers. Generic SaaS terms do not meet Art. 30 requirements.
AI failure not in ICT incident response plan
A significant AI system failure (incorrect credit decisions at scale, compliance reporting error) must be classified and reportable under DORA Art. 17.

Critical Gaps
No named SMCR accountable individual for AI
FCA requires a Senior Manager to take personal accountability for AI systems under SMCR. Absence is a direct regulatory exposure.
Bias testing not conducted before deployment

Customer-facing AI outputs not identified as AI-generated
FCA transparency expectations require consumers to know when AI influences decisions. Undisclosed AI use creates consumer harm risk.

GDPR Art.22 Readiness Score
CRITICAL GAPS
Critical Gaps
No Art. 22 determination documented
Organisations must actively determine whether their AI use constitutes automated decision-making under Art. 22. Absence of documented determination is itself a compliance gap.
No DPIA for AI profiling
Art. 35 requires a DPIA before processing that is likely to result in high risk. AI profiling of individuals almost always triggers this requirement.
No right-to-explanation process
Where Art. 22 applies, data subjects have the right to a meaningful explanation of the logic involved. Absence of an explanation process is a direct breach.
